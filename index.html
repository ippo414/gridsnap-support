Features

Everything you need.
Nothing you don't.

No cluttered preference panes, no learning curve. One gesture, a handful of thoughtful tools, and your screen falls into place.

01

Grid overlay

Summon a grid over your screen and drag to select cells — the active window snaps precisely into that region. Scroll to change columns, Shift-scroll for rows, from 1 to 24 each way, with adjustable cell gaps.

02

Window Pick Mode

Hold ⌘ while dragging to pick a region first, then click any window on screen — even one buried in the background — and it moves right into place.

03

Quick actions

Halves, thirds, two-thirds, quarters, maximize, center, and next/previous display — all one keystroke or a click in the menu bar away.

04

Layouts

Save your entire window arrangement and restore it anytime. Windows are matched by app and title, and template rules keep recurring setups automatic.

05

Multi-display

The grid appears on whichever screen your cursor is on. Move windows between displays while keeping their relative size and position.

06

Native & lightweight

Built in Swift. Lives quietly in your menu bar, launches at login if you want it to, and stays out of your Dock.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

Why does GridSnap need the Accessibility permission?

macOS only lets an app move or resize other apps' windows through its Accessibility API. GridSnap uses it for exactly that — nothing is read, recorded, or transmitted. You grant it once in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility.

My windows aren't moving. What should I check?

Make sure GridSnap is enabled under System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility, then relaunch the app. If it still misbehaves, open an issue and we'll take a look.

Does it work with multiple monitors?

Yes. The grid follows your cursor to whichever display it's on, and dedicated actions move windows between displays while preserving their relative size and position.
